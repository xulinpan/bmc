Dear Editors of BMC Bioinformatics,

Please consider my manuscript, "A leakage-controlled cold-start benchmark for drug-combination synergy classification on DrugComb v1.5", for publication as a Research Article in BMC Bioinformatics.

This work presents a reproducible, leakage-controlled benchmark for drug-combination synergy classification using the public DrugComb v1.5 dataset. It addresses a practical and under-reported question for computational pharmacology and bioinformatics: how much predictive signal is recoverable from pre-treatment compound and cell-context descriptors alone, without using any response-derived variable that would introduce target leakage?

Using 739,964 drug-combination experiments, I compare a transparent multinomial logistic-regression baseline with a class-weighted gradient-boosting (LightGBM) model under a grouped standard split and under leave-drug-out and leave-cell-line-out cold-start evaluations. The central finding is that standard-split performance substantially overstates deployment performance for unseen cellular backgrounds: balanced accuracy falls from 0.805 on the standard split to 0.482 under leave-cell-line-out evaluation, and permutation importance identifies cell-line identity as the dominant predictor. The study therefore supplies both a leakage-controlled lower-bound baseline and a cautionary evaluation framework for future molecular, graph-based and omics-integrated synergy models, and argues that cold-start evaluation should be reported alongside conventional splits.

The manuscript is well suited to BMC Bioinformatics because it provides a computational benchmark, a fully reproducible analysis workflow, and methodological guidance for modeling biological screening data. All analysis code and the metric outputs needed to regenerate the reported results are provided as supplementary material with this submission and will be deposited in a permanent public repository before final acceptance, and the study uses only publicly available, de-identified in vitro screening data; it does not involve new human-participant, human-tissue or animal experiments.
